NYC EMS Response Time

EECS 349 Machine Learning: Final Project
Northwestern University
Megan Conlon, Elana Stettin, Morgan Walker, Jay Valdillez

About

Our task is to predict the response time of an ambulance dispatched to a location in New York City based on attributes such as response times to emergencies nearby, zip code, time of year, time of day, atom (smallest area in the borough where incident occurred), and incident dispatch area as well as factors such as time of day and local average income in the zip code where the incident occurred. This project is important because it will predict how long it will take Emergency Medical Services to reach a location in New York City and also could show a correlation between response times and socioeconomic factors. This could help the city re-allocate resources to improve response times for certain areas of the city.
